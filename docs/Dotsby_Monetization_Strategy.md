# Dotsby Monetization Strategy

**Local-First iOS App | No Subscriptions Required**

---

## Philosophy

The free version must be **genuinely useful**â€”not a crippled demo. Parents will recommend Dotsby to other tired parents only if it solves their problem without friction. Monetization comes from delight, not desperation.

---

## Recommended Model: Paid Unlock + Tip Jar

### Why Not Subscription?

| Subscription Problems | Dotsby Reality |
|-----------------------|----------------|
| Requires ongoing server costs | No serversâ€”100% local |
| Users resent paying monthly for local apps | Parents are already subscription-fatigued |
| High churn after 1-2 years when baby grows | App has natural end-of-life |
| Harder to justify value monthly | One-time feels fair for utility app |

### Why Paid Unlock Works

- Parents use baby apps intensely for 12-24 months, then stop
- One-time purchase feels fair for a "tool"
- No guilt about forgetting to cancel
- Higher perceived value than $2.99/month

## Upgrade Triggers (Soft Prompts)

Show upgrade prompts only at **natural moments of delight or need**â€”never interrupt workflow.

| Trigger | Prompt |
|---------|--------|
| User adds 2nd baby | "Growing family? Unlock unlimited babies with Dotsby+" |
| User scrolls past 12-week history | "See your complete history with Dotsby+" |
| User tries to export | "Export your data with Dotsby+" |
| User takes 25th photo | "Never run out of spaceâ€”upgrade to Dotsby+" |
| After 30 days of active use | "Enjoying Dotsby? Consider leaving a tip ğŸ’›" |
| User shares app | Thank-you message + tip jar mention |

### Prompt Design Principles

1. **Never block functionality**â€”show prompt, then let them dismiss
2. **Never repeat the same prompt twice in a session**
3. **Never show prompts during active logging** (they're busy!)
4. **Always show what they'll get**, not what they're missing
5. **Make dismissing easy**â€”big "Not Now" button

## Alternative: "Pay What You Want" Launch

For initial traction, consider launching with:

1. **First 1,000 users:** Everything free (build reviews + word of mouth)
2. **After 1,000:** Introduce Dotsby+ at $9.99
3. **After 5,000:** Add tip jar

This builds goodwill and reviews before monetizing.

## What NOT to Do

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails |
|--------------|--------------|
| Ads | Parents hate ads. Destroys trust. Tiny revenue. |
| Aggressive paywalls | Creates resentment. Bad reviews. |
| Feature-gating core tracking | Makes free version useless. No referrals. |
| Time-limited trials | Pressure tactics feel gross for a baby app. |
| Multiple subscription tiers | Confusing. Parents don't have time to compare. |
| Consumable IAP (pay per export) | Feels nickel-and-dime. |
